Whey Protein

Whey protein is a mixture of proteins isolated from whey, the liquid material created as a by-product of cheese production. The proteins consist of α-lactalbumin, β-lactoglobulin, serum albumin and immunoglobulins.[1] Glycomacropeptide also makes up the third largest component but is not a protein. Whey protein is commonly marketed as a protein supplement.

Whey is left over when milk is coagulated during the process of cheese production. Coagulation can happen by adding acid or rennet. It is a 5% solution of lactose in water and contains the water-soluble proteins of milk as well as some lipid content. Processing can be done by simple drying, or the relative protein content can be increased by removing the lactose, lipids and other non-protein materials.

Main benefits of whey protein

  1. Supports muscle growth and repair:
    Whey is rich in essential amino acids, especially leucine, which plays a key role in muscle protein synthesis. That makes it ideal after workouts to help muscles recover and grow.

  2. Fast absorption
    Compared to other protein sources, whey is digested and absorbed quickly. This makes it especially useful post-exercise when your body needs nutrients fast.

  3. Helps with fat loss
    Whey protein can increase satiety (feeling full), which may reduce overall calorie intake. It also helps preserve muscle while you’re in a calorie deficit, which is important for maintaining metabolism.

  4. May help improve strength and performance
    Regular use alongside resistance training can lead to better strength gains and improved exercise performance.
